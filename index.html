what it does

Screenshots, actually worth keeping.

Grab a shot, draw on it, and find it again later — without ever opening a heavyweight editor.

Capture from the menu bar

Crosshair, previous area, timed, fullscreen, window, or a menu — one click from the bar.

Annotate with real objects

Arrows, boxes, text, highlighter, blur — every mark is selectable and movable, not baked in.

Automatic history

Every markup is saved automatically, with your recent shots one tap away in the menu bar.

Export real layered files

Save an editable document, or export layered PDF, PNG, and SVG — nothing flattened by surprise.

how it works

Three steps, no friction

It lives in your menu bar and stays out of the way until you need to grab something.

1

Capture from the bar

Pick a capture mode from the menu bar — crosshair for a region, or grab a whole window. Your shot opens straight in a zoomable editor.

clipandnote

Fullscreen Clip⌥⌘3
2

Mark it up

Drop arrows, boxes, text and highlights — each one stays a live object you can move, restyle, or delete. Blur the bits that shouldn't be public.

3

Copy, save, or export

Send it straight to the clipboard for a quick paste, save an editable document, or export a layered file. It hands off to clipandcue as your latest copy — without flooding its history.
