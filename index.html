what it does

Screenshots,
actually worth keeping.

Grab a shot, draw on it, and find it again later,
without ever opening a heavyweight editor.

Capture from the menu bar

Crosshair, previous area, timed, fullscreen, window, or a menu. One click from the bar.

Annotate with real objects

Arrows, boxes, text, highlighter, blur. Every mark is selectable and movable, not baked in.

Automatic history

Every markup is saved automatically, with your recent shots one tap away in the menu bar.

Output multi-page PDFs

Tick the markups you want, drag them into order, and export one PDF with optional page numbers.

Export real layered files

Save an editable document, or export layered PDF and SVG — or a flat PNG when you need one. Your vector exports keep every mark.

Grab text from a screenshot

Pull any text out of a screenshot with on-device OCR. Selected lines copy straight to your clipboard.

how it works

Three steps, no friction

It lives in your menu bar and stays out of the way
until you need to grab something.

1

Capture from the bar

Pick a capture mode from the menu bar (Crosshair Clip is ⌥⌘4), or grab a whole window. Your shot opens straight in the editor.

Mark it up
